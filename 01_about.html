About


I have a MSc. Eng. degree in Organic Chemistry (2002) from Silesian University of Technology (Poland) and a PhD in Theoretical Chemistry (2010) from Utah State University (US). During my doctorate, I also spent a year (2008) as a Visiting Researcher at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (Spain). In 2011, after finishing one-year Optional Practical Training at the Biotechnology Company in San Francisco (US), I relocated to Japan to become a Postdoctoral Researcher (2011 – 2016) and then a Research Scientist (2016 – 2020) at the RIKEN Yokohama Institute.

Since August 2020, I am a Bioinformatics Associate Professor at the Advanced Medical Research Center at Yokohama City University and a Visiting Scientist at RIKEN Center of Inegrative Medical Sciences (IMS). Our Bioinformatics Lab is using a variety of computational software and tools (R statistical software, Linux, etc.) and statistical methods to analyze medically relevant Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) data. I am also teaching Bioinformatics courses to help medical students and experimental researchers.
